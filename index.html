Steps In IVF Process

Step 1 Your doctor prescribes a course of drugs for you to take to stimulate your ovaries into hopefully producing 12 to 15 mature eggs.
Step 2 You receive a light general anaesthetic for a short procedure where your doctor retrieves your eggs using an ultrasound guided technique.
Step 3 On the day of egg collection, your partner or choice of donor provides a sperm sample.The embryologist puts sperm and eggs together in the lab and, if all goes well, the eggs fertilise and early embryo development begins.
Step 4 Embryos at each subsequent stage of development are grown in media similar to what they would encounter as they make their way down the tube into the uterus.
Step 5 Two to five days after egg collection, your doctor places the eggs in the uterus. Two weeks later you'll be tested to see if the treatment worked and if you're pregnant.
